FAQ

Is this one course or a full combo?
It’s a full combo access. You’re not buying one isolated course. You’re unlocking Karyna’s 5 foundation programs, so you can start with what your body needs now and switch anytime.
I can barely touch my toes. Is this too advanced?
No. That is a normal starting point.
You don’t need to already be flexible to begin. The programs are designed to help your body build range gradually through mobility, activation, strength support, and controlled stretching.
Can I do 2 lessons in one day?
Yes. You can do 2 lessons in one day if your body feels ready — especially when you combine one more intense session with one lighter mobility or beginner session.
Good combinations:
Front Split + Back Mobility
Middle Split + Beginner Course
Powerfit Body + Back Mobility
Split lesson + light mobility lesson

Just avoid forcing deep stretching when your body feels tired.
Is 15 minutes really enough?
Yes - when the session is structured correctly.
The Zubalenok Method focuses on sequence, activation, mobility, and control. A short, focused lesson can be more effective than a long random stretching session because your body is prepared before it is asked to open.
The key is consistency and the right order of work, not endless training time.
I’m 40+. Will this work for my body?
Yes. Many Zubalenok students start in their 40s, 50s, and beyond.
You don’t need to train like a teenager to improve your mobility and flexibility. You need the right sequence, controlled progress, modifications, and consistency.
Always listen to your body and choose the range that feels safe for you.
Do I need equipment?
You can start with simple home equipment.
A mat is recommended. Some lessons may use props like blocks, a chair, or resistance bands to help with support, alignment, or deeper control.
You don’t need a gym. Everything is designed for home training.
